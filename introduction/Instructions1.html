At the beginning of Part 2, you will play in a group of two with another participant. In each round, you will be randomly matched to another participant. That means you may not be playing with the same participant every round.

You and the other participant play the role of retailers who sell a single (fictional) product.

In each round, you and the other retailer face customer demand for your product. Customer demand varies between 0 and 200 units of the product. All demand between 0 and 200 is equally likely.

In each round, the demand you and the other retailer face is drawn randomly and independently. That means that demand varies between rounds. The demand you and the other retailer face in the same round can be different.

In each round, before demand is randomly drawn, you and the other retailer can order product units from a supplier at a procurement cost of 20 ECU per unit and sell units to customers at a retail price of 40 ECU per unit.

If you order more units than customers demand, you have excess inventory. You cannot sell excess inventory from the current round in the next round.

If you order fewer units than customers demand, you have excess demand.
