To deal with excess inventory and excess demand, you can decide to engage in a transfer with the other retailer.

If both you and the other retailer decide to engage in a transfer, this will happen:

If you face excess demand:

  1. If the other retailer has excess inventory, they will transfer it to you.
  2. If your excess demand is higher than or equal to the other retailer's excess inventory, all the other retailer's excess inventory is transferred to you.
  3. If your excess demand is lower than the other retailer's excess inventory, only the units required to satisfy your demand are transferred. You can satisfy customer demand fully.

For every unit transferred to you, you pay a transfer cost of 12 ECU to the other retailer.

If you face excess inventory:

  1. If the other retailer has excess demand, you will transfer your inventory to them.
  2. If your excess inventory is lower than or equal to the other retailer's excess demand, all your excess inventory is transferred to them.
  3. If your excess inventory is greater than the other retailer's excess demand, only the units required to satisfy the other retailer's excess demand are transferred to them.

For every unit transferred to the other retailer, you receive a transfer price of 12 ECU and a transfer fee of 2 ECU per unit is deducted from your account.

For every unit in your excess inventory that is not transferred to the other retailer, you receive a salvage price of 10 ECU.

If only you or the other retailer decide to engage in a transfer, or if neither one of you decides to engage in a transfer, this will happen:

  1. No transfer of units takes place.
  2. If you face excess demand, none of your excess demand is met.
  3. If you face excess inventory, all is salvaged at a salvage price of 10 ECU per unit.
