At the beginning of Part 3, you will play in a group of two with another participant. In each round, you will be randomly matched to another participant. That means you may not be playing with the same participant every round.

You and the other participant play the role of retailers who sell a single (fictional) product.

For each round, you and the other retailer face customer demand for your product. Customer demands vary between 0 and 200 units of the product.

{#All demands between 0 and 200 are equally likely.#}

For each round, the demands you and the other retailer face are drawn randomly and independently. That means that demands vary between rounds. The demands you and the other retailer face in the same round can be different.

For each round, before demands are randomly drawn, you and the other retailer's task is to order product units from a supplier at a procurement cost of 20 ECU per unit and sell units to customers at a retail price of 40 ECU per unit.

If you order more units than customers demand, you have excess inventory. You cannot sell excess inventory from the current round in the next round.

If you order fewer units than customers demand, you have excess demand.
